Persona For power User

Persona For Casual User

Name: Amanda James

Age:40

Sex: Female

Ethnicity: African Americans

Occupation: Optical Engineer, Tenured Research Professor, Owner of Celestia, a government R and D contracting company.

Technology: 2014 Lenovo ThinkPad X1 Carbon | 14inch, 2560x1440 touch screen | 1.6GHz Intel Core i5-4200U | 8GB DDR3 SDRAM 1600MHz | 128 GB SSD | Windows 8.1(64-bit), Celestron NexStar 8SE Schmidt Telescope, Google Pixel (1st gen), Samsung Galaxy Tablet A 10.5 in, 32GB, WiFi enabled.

Attitude: Friendly person, loves to teach especially about space and stars. She is passionate about space and space technologies. She stargazes and takes photos as a way to slow down and reset after long work weeks. She loves hosting star parties at her home in Tijeras.

Frustrations: She doesn't often have time to socialize and connect with people outside of her professional circles. The lack of access community members have to information about astronomy, and general lack of awareness around astronomy. She is also eternally frustrated with the exclusivity of local astronomy clubs, and wants to host open events that everyone can feel comfortable at and attend.

Goals: Amanda wants to use the site to build a social network, host events she's passionate about and foster her love of teaching..

User Story: Amanda wants to quickly post an open invitation to a star party she is hosting at her house with colleagues.

Use Case: After a casual meeting with collegues, Amanda wants to find a website to post an open invitation for a star party she and her collegues are hosting. She creates an open event invitation for a star party that she can also share with her facebook community.

Description:

Name of user and user role: Amanda James, physics and astronomy expert/party organizer, teacher, no kids.

Useage precondition: Amanda has already signed up for the site.

Interaction Flow

  • Amanda enters the website's URL.
  • Server returns personalized splash page because her pw software automatically logs her in.
  • Amanda clicks the "+ event" button.
  • Server returns a pre-established event post form (includes name of event, location, date, description).
  • Amanda fills in all of the fields and clicks "post event".
  • Server takes form information and sorts it into event table.
  • Amanda sees event post on website and realizes she's made a mistake in the time and clicks "edit event".
  • Server brings event post form back.
  • Amanda adjusts event time.
  • Server changes previous time input to new time input.
  • Amanda sees changes reflected after she saves changes to the event.
  • Server prompts Amanda to share URL via email, facebook, twitter, SMS.
  • Amanda copies the event URL into a facebook post, now all her friends know about it.
